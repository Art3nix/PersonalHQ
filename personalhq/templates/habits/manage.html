1
The One Percent Rule

Habits are the compound interest of self-improvement. Consistency is key. Getting 1% better every day yields massive results. You do not become better overnight.

2
Identity Over Outcomes

The goal is not the habit itself, the goal is the behavior change and the new identity. Every checked-off habit is a vote cast for that new identity.

3
Build The System

Winners and losers have the exact same goals. You do not rise to the level of your goals, you fall to the level of your systems. Winners win by having better systems that enforce them to work consistently. Focus on building the system that guarantees the result.

4
The Habit Scorecard

Our brains operate primarily on cues and much of what we do is automatic. To change a habit, you must first be aware of it. Write down everything you do in a day. Score it with a plus for positive, a minus for negative, and an equals sign for neutral. This identifies habits to break and habits to stack upon.

5
Master The Crossroads

A few key moments throughout the day dictate the majority of our actions. One small choice determines whether you spend the next 30 minutes reading or doomscrolling. Master the crossroads.

The Creation Playbook

Reduce friction. Scale any new habit down until it takes less than 2 minutes. Master the art of showing up. At least show up. Most people dont even spend a minute to work on themselves.

The best trigger for a new habit is the completion of an old one. Use this simple formula: After my current habit, I will do my new habit. Link an action you want to do with an action you need to do.

1
The Cue

To build a habit, make it obvious. To break a bad habit, make it invisible and remove the cue entirely from your environment.

2
The Craving

To build a habit, make it attractive. To break a bad habit, make it unattractive and highlight the benefits of avoiding it.

3
The Response

To build a habit, make it easy. To break a bad habit, make it difficult and increase friction. Put your phone in another room and let laziness work for you.

4
The Reward

What is immediately rewarded is repeated. To build a habit, make it satisfying. To break a bad habit, make it unsatisfying by adding an immediate cost to failure.
