1
The Ultimate Deadline

When faced with a terminal diagnosis, wealth becomes instantly worthless. The only thing that matters is how you spent your time. You must adopt this perspective now, not when you have months left to live. The ultimate goal is to reach your final moments with absolutely zero regrets about the things you did not do.

2
Die With Zero

Money is simply fuel for experiences. If you die with two million dollars in the bank, you traded thousands of hours of your healthy life working for paper you never used. That is a massive failure in life optimization.

3
The Memory Dividend

Experiences compound in your mind just like money compounds in the market. When you take a great trip at age 25, you get to remember it for the next 50 years. Investing heavily in experiences when you are young yields the highest lifetime return.

4
The Health Curve

Money has the highest utility when you are healthy. Do not save hiking the Alps for when you are 70 and have bad knees. You must actively use your wealth to buy back your time and fund your experiences while your body can actually handle them.

Plot Experience

Die With Zero

1
Brainstorm Without Limits

Write down everything you want to do before you die. Do not filter yourself based on money or logistics right now. Get the raw ideas into the system first. You will organize the funding later.

2
Be Specific

Saying you want to travel more is just a wish. Saying you want to hike the Camino de Santiago with your best friend is a plotted experience. Use the details box to write down exactly who you are going with to make it real.

3
The Real Risk

If an experience feels too risky or expensive, write down the absolute worst case scenario. You will quickly realize the risk is exaggerated. The only true risk is reaching the end of your life and regretting the things you did not do. Do not let fear stop you.

Add Time Bucket

Die With Zero

1
Box Your Time

A traditional bucket list fails because it lacks deadlines. Estimate your life expectancy to find your true end date, then divide your remaining years into strict five or ten year buckets. This forces you to schedule your life and creates the urgency needed to act.

2
Define The Theme

Every decade of your life has a different physical and mental reality. Give this bucket a specific theme like Career Building or Peak Adventure. This helps you figure out exactly what experiences belong here.

3
The Survival Threshold

This is the absolute minimum wealth required to survive. The formula is your annual cost of living multiplied by your years left to live, multiplied by 0.7. Once you cross this number, stop grinding and start funding these buckets.
