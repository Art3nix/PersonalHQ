The Inbox Guide

A Safe Place For Your Mind

Your mind is beautifully designed to create ideas, not to hold onto them. Every lingering thought or forgotten task quietly drains your energy. Think of this inbox as a pressure valve. The exact moment you write a worry or an idea down here, you are whispering to your brain: "I've got this. It is safe to let it go."

  • 1
    Just Get It Out When a thought strikes, dump it here immediately. Don't worry about organizing it or making it sound smart. Just get it out of your head so you can stay present in your life.
  • 2
    Clear The Room To keep your mind at peace, make a habit of clearing this list regularly. Think of it as a temporary waiting room, not a permanent storage closet.
  • X
    Let Things Go Not every passing thought is a mandate. If an idea no longer excites you or serves you, just delete it. Freeing up mental space is a beautiful thing.

The 2-Minute Relief

If this thought takes less than two minutes to resolve (like sending a quick text or paying a bill), don't even bother scheduling it. Just do it right now, enjoy that little burst of momentum, and delete the thought!

2
Define the Outcome

Vague tasks cause stress because your brain doesn't know what "done" looks like. Change a vague word like "Taxes" to a clear outcome like "File 2025 tax return." Then, just ask yourself what the very first physical step is (like opening the spreadsheet) so you have an easy starting line.

3
Give It a Home

Where does this idea belong? If it's a project, send it to Deep Work. If it's a dream for the future, make it an Experience. If it's just a feeling, save it in your Journal. Every piece of your mind deserves a proper home.
