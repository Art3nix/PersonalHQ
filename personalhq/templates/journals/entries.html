1
Ask Open Questions

Instead of asking "Was today a good day?", try asking "What brought me energy today?" Open-ended questions give your mind the space it needs to actually wander and reflect, rather than just giving a quick yes or no.

2
Focus on Growth

Great prompts do not just look backwards, they help you move forward. Asking yourself "What is one small thing I can do better tomorrow?" turns your reflection into gentle, inspiring action.

3
Keep It Kind

Your journal is a safe space, not a courtroom. Write prompts that invite self-compassion rather than harsh judgment. You are here to understand yourself and grow, not to interrogate yourself.
