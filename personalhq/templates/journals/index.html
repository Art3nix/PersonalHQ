1
Clear Your Head

Think of your journal as a pressure valve for your brain. Writing things down helps move swirling anxieties out of your head and onto the page where you can actually deal with them. It is the fastest way to stop overthinking and start finding clarity.

2
Inbox vs Journal

Don’t mistake your journal for a to-do list—there is a separate Inbox for that. Your Inbox handles the logistics of what you need to do, but your Journal handles the reality of who you are becoming. Keeping them separate means your journal stays a stress-free space for reflection.

3
One Space, One Vibe

Try to give each journal a strict, narrow focus. If you mix your work notes with your personal gratitudes, your brain gets confused about what "mode" it should be in. Keeping them separate makes it way easier to focus—and it helps your future AI assistant understand you better.
