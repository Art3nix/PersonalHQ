Deep vs Shallow Work

Deep work pushes your cognitive capabilities to their absolute limit. Shallow work is just logistical busywork like checking emails or Slack. Do not confuse the two. Only queue up true deep work here.

2
The Focus Equation

High quality work equals time spent multiplied by intensity of focus. By maximizing your intensity, you can drastically reduce the time needed to produce elite results.

3
Drain the Shallows

Shallow work consists of minor, logistical tasks that do not create real value. You must give every single hour of your day a job. When you map out your schedule, you instantly expose exactly how much time you are wasting on busywork. Constrain the shallows so you can protect your focus.

4
The Shutdown Ritual

At the end of the workday, you must shut down completely. No checking email after dinner. Disconnect. This ensures your mental energy is fully recharged for tomorrow.

The Deep Work Playbook

1
The Monastic Philosophy

Isolate yourself completely for long periods. This is best for extreme focus, like writing a book or doing heavy research.

2
The Bimodal Philosophy

Divide your time clearly. Dedicate specific full days to deep work and leave the rest of the week open for shallow obligations.

3
The Rhythmic Philosophy

Generate a simple daily habit. Do 90 minutes every single morning before checking your phone. This is the most practical method for a standard career.

4
The Journalistic Philosophy

Fit deep work into whatever free time you have. Shift into intense focus instantly like a journalist on a tight deadline. This requires practice.

1
Build The Ritual

Decide exactly where you will work and how long you will work before you start the timer. Define exactly what done looks like. Leave your phone in another room.

2
Zero Context Switching

Pick one cognitively demanding task. Do not check email or Slack. Multitasking is a myth. Every time you switch tabs you leave attention residue behind. This ruins your cognitive performance.

3
Embrace The Boredom

Focus is a muscle. When your brain craves a quick dopamine hit, recognize the urge, ignore it, and push through. That friction is your brain actually rewiring.
